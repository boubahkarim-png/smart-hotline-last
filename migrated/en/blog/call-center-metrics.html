Key Call Center Metrics Every Business Should Track

February 28, 2024

Discover the most important KPIs for measuring call center performance and how to use them to improve your customer service operations.

Why measure performance?

Average Response Time (ART)

This KPI measures the average time between receiving a call and an agent answering it. Fast ART is crucial for customer satisfaction and can be improved through effective scheduling and proper training.

Abandon Rate

The percentage of calls where customers hang up before being served. High abandon rate indicates capacity issues or long wait times. Monitoring this KPI helps adjust staffing and improve customer experience.

Average Resolution Time (ART)

The average time required to completely resolve a customer request. Shorter resolution time improves customer satisfaction and reduces operational costs. CRM integration can help speed up this process.

First Contact Resolution (FCR)

The percentage of requests resolved during the first contact with the customer. High FCR means increased efficiency and customer satisfaction. Investing in training and the right tools improves this crucial KPI.

Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT)

Direct measures of customer satisfaction through post-interaction surveys. This qualitative KPI provides valuable insights into customer perception and identifies improvement areas.
